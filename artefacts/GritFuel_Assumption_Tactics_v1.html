Principle

A risky assumption without a validation path, mitigation tactics, and tripwire signals is just a worry on a slide. Every tactic below is one of three types: something we do regardless (mitigation), something we run to find out if the assumption is true (validation), or something we watch for to know if it's breaking (signal). The goal isn't to eliminate risk — it's to make each assumption observable and reversible.

01
Founders will pay for a system they can't see.
Risk type · Emotional buy-in · Highest leverage
If this breaks
No deals close
Root cause

The OS is 5 tools in a backend loop. Tools are rational; growth is emotional. A founder signing €3.5–8k/mo needs something visceral to buy, not a process diagram. Today the Compound Score and Dashboard are described as outputs — they need to be the product in the buyer's mind.

Validation approach

Show 10 prospects two pitch variants: (a) "here's our OS and how it works" (b) "here's your Compound Score and what it's telling you this month." Track: time-to-first-question, emotional language in response, close rate. If (b) wins 3:1 or better on engagement, the hypothesis is confirmed — the Score is the product, everything else is infrastructure.

Tactics
T1.1
Make the Compound Score the product's face
Dashboard hero, monthly email subject line, first line of every Playbook, first artifact a prospect sees in sales. "Your Compound Score is 73 — up from 68 last month" becomes the conversational unit.
Impact: HighEffort: Low
T1.2
Pre-sales Dashboard demo (10 min)
First 10 minutes of every sales call: walk a real (or anonymised beta) client's Dashboard. Lets prospect experience the product before hearing any pitch. Cuts abstract-system perception instantly.
Impact: HighEffort: Low
T1.3
"Month 0" diagnostic deliverable
Before full onboarding, offer a standalone €500–€1k diagnostic: we ingest their existing data and produce a baseline Compound Score + one Playbook page. Tangible artifact, low commitment, converts at 60%+.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
T1.4
Visible monthly Growth State email
Client-facing email on the 1st of every month: Score, trend, top 3 initiatives, Playbook summary. Shareable format — founders forward it to board members, which becomes earned distribution.
Impact: MediumEffort: Low
T1.5
Score-driven case studies
Every case study leads with the Score trajectory: "Client X went from 41 (Pivot) to 78 (Accelerate) in six months." Anchors the Score as the metric customers are buying.
Impact: MediumEffort: Medium
Signals the assumption is breaking
Prospects don't mention the Score unprompted after call 2
Sales calls keep circling back to "what do you actually do?"
Existing clients don't reference the Score in renewal conversations
Dashboard engagement (MAU) < 2 sessions/month per client
02
SMEs with €50–500k budgets want a system, not a relationship.
Risk type · GTM motion · Channel design
If this breaks
Wrong channel
Root cause

EU mid-market buying behaviour is overwhelmingly relationship-driven: intros, repeat vendors, "my friend recommended." The OS is the rational justification — the trust layer is what closes. If GritFuel leads with product and omits the human, the pitch reads as SaaS and loses to the fractional CMO who had drinks with them last Thursday.

Validation approach

Run two parallel sales funnels for 90 days. Funnel A: inbound/product-led — website + demo video + booking calendar. Funnel B: founder-led — Ruben + co-founders doing intros and first meetings personally. Track conversion, sales-cycle length, and 6-month retention. The ratio tells us the real balance of system-vs-relationship in our ICP.

Tactics
T2.1
Founder-led sales for first 20 clients
Ruben + co-founders in every discovery call until client 20. Relationship is the product during beta. Data from these calls feeds every other tactic in this doc.
Impact: HighEffort: Low
T2.2
Named strategist model, publicly visible
Every client is introduced to their strategist by name + photo + bio at onboarding. The OS scales; the strategist relationship is what clients refer to when they refer us on.
Impact: HighEffort: Low
T2.3
Beta cohort community
Private Slack/WhatsApp for beta clients. Monthly founder office hours. Peer intros across the cohort. Converts clients into relationship-rich advocates who sell for us in their own networks.
Impact: MediumEffort: Medium
T2.4
Public founder POV content
Weekly LinkedIn posts, monthly long-form essay, one podcast appearance per month — all authored by named founders, not the GritFuel brand account. Prospects feel they already know us before first call.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
T2.5
Two-funnel test (see validation)
Run inbound-product vs. founder-led sales funnels in parallel for a quarter. Let the data decide where to invest. If founder-led wins by 2x, GTM reshapes around relationship; if inbound wins, we scale the system layer.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
Signals the assumption is breaking
Inbound funnel converts < 2%; founder-led converts > 25%
"Who will I work with?" is the #1 question in sales
Clients reference their strategist's name when renewing, not the OS
Referrals come through people, never through the website
03
The credit model reduces friction.
Risk type · Pricing legibility · Operational
If this breaks
Churn + confusion
Root cause

Credits are a clean unit internally (1 credit = €25 output) but an abstraction externally. Every budget-minded client will ask "what is a credit worth, really?" and answer it themselves — sometimes wrongly. If clients feel they're being billed in a currency they can't audit, trust breaks even if value is being delivered.

Validation approach

In the first 10 prospect calls, explicitly ask: "The credit model — does this make it easier or harder for you to understand what you're getting?" Listen for the word "complicated." Then, post-onboarding for first 5 clients, measure time-to-first-confusion: how many days before a credit-related question is asked? < 14 days = real problem.

Tactics
T3.1
Always show credits AND euros
Every Playbook line, every invoice, every Dashboard allocation shows both: "Landing page rebuild — 12 credits (€300)". Dual-unit pricing removes the translation burden entirely.
Impact: HighEffort: Low
T3.2
Outcome-named credit bundles
Instead of "5 credits," name packaged deliverables: "Launch Pack" (10 credits), "SEO Sprint" (15 credits), "Brand Refresh" (25 credits). Clients buy outcomes, credits become invisible plumbing.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
T3.3
Real-time credit tracker in Dashboard
Live balance, forecast use, rollover countdown, utilization by channel. Makes the model inspectable at any moment — trust through transparency.
Impact: MediumEffort: Medium
T3.4
First Playbook pre-allocates 100%
Month 1 Playbook arrives with all credits already allocated. Client only has to approve, not decide. Removes cognitive load during the fragile onboarding window.
Impact: HighEffort: Low
T3.5
Flat-fee bailout tier
For clients who structurally resist abstraction, offer "Core Flat" at €3,500/mo with a fixed pre-defined scope (no credits). Loses some flexibility upside, but recovers a segment otherwise lost to confusion. Experiment, not default.
Impact: MediumEffort: High
Signals the assumption is breaking
Credit questions dominate the first 30 days with new clients
Clients don't use their full monthly credit allocation by month 3
Support tickets tagged "pricing" or "billing" > 10% of total
NPS dips within 3 days of monthly invoice
04
No agency can copy this in the 18-month head-start window.
Risk type · Defensibility · Competitive
If this breaks
Margin compression
Root cause

The OS is copyable. S4 Capital, WPP's SME arms, Dept, Accenture Song all have the capital and talent to build a GritFuel clone in 12–18 months once the model is validated. The data-accumulation moat is real but slow — it takes 6–12 months per client for switching cost to become meaningful. Defensibility is mostly head-start, not inherent.

Validation approach

Quarterly competitive sweep: (a) track "growth operating system" and adjacent search volume, (b) scan S4/WPP/Dept/Accenture job posts for tell-tale roles (e.g. "subscription growth lead"), (c) monitor domain registrations in the category, (d) sample-buy competitor outputs where possible. Rate of convergence > expected = accelerate data-moat and category-branding tactics.

Tactics
T4.1
Own the category name
Make "Growth Operating System" and GritFuel conceptually inseparable. Whitepapers, conference talks, podcast appearances that define the category on our terms. Copycats arrive to a landscape we've already shaped.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
T4.2
Aggressive public thinking — not product
Publish Compounding Engine logic, OS architecture, pricing philosophy openly. Counter-intuitive: transparency about method creates trust, not imitation risk. Imitators still need talent, data, and relationships — none of which are in the whitepaper.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
T4.3
Accelerate client count, not ARPU
Optimize for client logo count in Y1–Y2 over revenue-per-client. Each client contributes data to the moat. 25 clients at Core tier is a stronger defensive position than 10 at Performance tier. Re-read the SOM plan with this lens.
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
T4.4
Senior strategist talent lock-up
Identify the 10 senior strategists in EU most aligned with the OS model. Contract them, advise them, hire them, or build MOUs before competitors realize the category exists. Talent is the real binding constraint — not the tooling.
Impact: HighEffort: High
T4.5
Exclusive accelerator / VC partnerships
Lock 3–5 exclusive partnerships with accelerators or seed funds who refer their entire portfolio. Creates a structural inbound channel competitors can't easily replicate. See VC partner deck for existing framework.
Impact: HighEffort: High
T4.6
Trade-secret & IP discipline
Formally document Compounding Engine scoring methodology, Router logic, and Playbook templates as trade secrets. Consider modest patent filings where defensible. Won't stop a clone but raises the cost of blatant copying.
Impact: MediumEffort: Medium
Signals the assumption is breaking
A large agency launches a monthly subscription tier for SMEs
"Growth operating system" search volume triples YoY from non-GritFuel sources
Agency job posts mention "compound" / "credits" / "subscription" growth models
Prospects mention a competitor offering a similar structure in discovery calls
