Internal Review

Pricing Tier Review & Suggestions

Five issues with the current tier structure, each with a concrete fix. The goal is to tighten the feature progression, align naming with GritFuel OS, and make the credit model visible — without changing the tier logic or taglines, which are strong.

The Issues
ISSUE 01
Redundant features in Growth and Performance
Growth says "Everything in Core, plus" but then re-lists "Monthly Growth System" and "Strategic alignment rhythm" — items already in Core. Performance does the same with "Monthly Growth System" and "Fractional CMO oversight". This reads as a copy error and undermines trust.
Fix: Remove re-listed items from Growth and Performance. Only list genuinely new capabilities per tier.
ISSUE 02
"Monthly Growth System" is a weak, generic name
The playbook is GritFuel's primary delivery mechanism and proprietary IP. "Monthly Growth System" doesn't communicate that. It sounds like any other agency's monthly report. The product is already called the "Monthly Growth Playbook" in all other materials.
Fix: Rename to "Monthly Growth Playbook" (or "Compounding Growth Playbook" at Performance tier to signal the intelligence layer activating).
ISSUE 03
The credit model is invisible
Credits are the core commercial mechanic — the thing that makes the model transparent and client-aligned. They're not mentioned anywhere on the pricing page. Clients have no way to understand how output scales with tier, or what "more" looks like in practice.
Fix: Add a credit band indicator per tier (e.g., "40–60 credits / month" → "80–120 credits / month" → "140+ credits / month"). This shows progression without publishing a fixed price.
ISSUE 04
The Compounding Engine is missing entirely
The Compounding Engine is GritFuel's biggest differentiator — the thing that gets smarter every month. It doesn't appear anywhere in the pricing tiers. This is the IP that justifies staying long-term, and it's not even hinted at in the feature lists.
Fix: Surface it as a named feature in Growth ("Compounding growth engine — active") and make it more explicit in Performance ("Full compounding engine + forecasting").
ISSUE 05
Core tier uses passive, vague language
"Focused execution support" and "Performance dashboards" are weak. GritFuel's tone is active: "we build, execute, optimise." Even the entry tier should feel like a purposeful growth system, not a support service. "Dashboards" also doesn't match the product name ("Live growth dashboard").
Fix: Rename to "Focused channel execution" and "Live growth dashboard" to match the OS language and use active framing.
BONUS
Consider: one "not included" signal per tier
A single explicit exclusion per tier (e.g., Core: "Fractional CMO not included") creates a natural upgrade tension without being aggressive. It's a common SaaS pattern that makes the value of the next tier immediately obvious — especially for visitors who are evaluating Core vs. Growth.
Fix: Add one greyed-out "not included" item at the bottom of Core and Growth. Keep it to one — don't overdo it.

Additional Considerations
On "Structured experimentation framework" — worth a rename
This is the right capability to feature in Growth, but the name is internal-sounding. Consider: "Monthly A/B testing framework" or "Structured growth experiments" — slightly more tangible for a prospect scanning the page quickly.
On credit bands — don't publish fixed numbers publicly yet
The "40–60 credits / month" style indicator communicates scale and progression without locking you to a number. During Beta phase this is especially useful — you can adjust credit depth per client while the tier label stays consistent. If you prefer to keep it off the page entirely, at minimum consider adding it to the sales flow (scoping call, proposal) so prospects understand the volume difference.
On "Monthly strategy session" vs "Strategic alignment rhythm"
"Rhythm" sounds like agency jargon. "Monthly strategy session" is concrete — it describes an actual thing that happens. If the cadence is different per tier (e.g., bi-weekly in Performance), that's worth surfacing: "Monthly strategy session" → "Bi-weekly strategy sessions" as an upgrade signal.
One open question: does Core include the Compounding Engine?
Currently the Compounding Engine is listed as a Growth-and-above feature in this mockup. The argument for that: Core clients have less data history, and the engine becomes more valuable over time — so making it a Growth feature creates a natural upsell trigger at month 2–3. But you could also argue it should be in all tiers (it just outputs less on limited data). Worth a deliberate decision before locking the page.
