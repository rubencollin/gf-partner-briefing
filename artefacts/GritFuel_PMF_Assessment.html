Internal positioning note · April 2026

PMF Assessment & Positioning Recommendations

A working assessment of GritFuel's product-market fit state and the positioning calls that follow. This is the companion memo behind the Business Model Canvas, JTBD/VPC, and Founder/CEO DeepDive — the "why these choices" document.

This note exists to answer three questions partners should be able to ground the go-live in: (1) Do we have product-market fit? (2) If not, what's missing? (3) What positioning choices follow from the answer? The canvases are the primary source; this memo is the rationale.

1. PMF Assessment

1.1 Where we are on the Sean Ellis curve

Classic PMF signal: ≥40% of users saying they'd be "very disappointed" if the product disappeared. We have no quantitative read yet — the Growth Dashboard, Compounding Engine, and Monthly Playbook are pre-launch. But qualitative anchors are legible enough to rate each PMF input honestly.

1.2 The honest read

We do not yet have PMF. We have pre-PMF signal: a problem founders name the same way across conversations ("I don't have a growth function"), a solution shape (a Growth OS + monthly Playbook) that plausibly resolves it, and a set of delivery partners (.frank, Bluegyfu, BFL) that can execute the work. What we do not have is a single client running a full monthly cycle against the product at the price.

The precise state
Problem-solution fit: plausible. Product-market fit: unverified. Founder-channel fit: untested. The 22 April soft launch moves us from "built" to "in front of the inner circle," and the 18 May official launch moves us from "in front of a circle" to "running" — it's the first condition under which PMF can be measured at all.

2. The positioning problem we were solving

Prior framing described GritFuel as a "brandformance agency" — implying embedded service delivery with a brand and performance layer. That positioning has three problems:

  • Category overlap. "Brandformance" is already used by other shops in the EU market. Claiming it makes us hard to differentiate.
  • Pricing ceiling. Agencies are priced on retainer bands shaped by market comps. A new shop enters at the bottom of the curve; existing shops are priced ~40–60% higher for the same output shape.
  • Delivery shape. The partnership is already four-way (GritFuel + .frank + Bluegyfu + BFL). An "agency" framing hides the orchestration layer — which is the thing that is actually novel.

3. Positioning Recommendations

R1 · Category
Position as a Growth Operating System, not an agency.
A product the founder logs into, with the agency work happening behind the product, not instead of it. This reframes every downstream choice: pricing becomes subscription + credits (not retainer), delivery becomes orchestrated multi-partner (not single-shop), and the primary interaction surface becomes software (the five OS tools + Monthly Playbook), not account-management. Why: "Growth OS" is an open category in the EU SMB/scale-up segment — no one has claimed it. Cost: we carry the burden of actually having a product, which introduces the product-engineering capability gap called out elsewhere in this briefing.
R2 · Umbrella & tagline
Three-layer tagline system — resolves the petrol-brand collision.
Umbrella: "Human thinking. Relentless execution." Descriptor: "The growth operating system." Companion: "Growth that compounds. Visibly." A layered system is needed because "GritFuel" alone triggers fuel/petrol associations in EU markets — the descriptor disambiguates instantly. Why: single-tagline systems force a choice between category-naming and emotional-resonance; a three-layer system lets us lead with whichever fits the surface (investor deck: umbrella; sales page: descriptor; social: companion).
R3 · Revenue architecture
Two-stream — fixed subscription plus credit pool.
Stream 1 (fixed): €3,500 / €5,250 / €8,000 per month for OS access, Monthly Playbook, strategic oversight. High-margin, covers overhead. Stream 2 (credits): a monthly credit pool the client spends against the ALC Menu — 75% cost-share to partners, €25/credit output value. Low-margin but high-volume. Why: a single-stream retainer collapses either into an agency (low margin, high overhead) or a SaaS (too thin to include real delivery). The two-stream model lets us be a product and deliver service without one cannibalising the other.
R4 · ICP & segment
EU/NL seed-to-Series-A founders, 3–30 person teams, post-traction pre-scale.
Specifically: founders with real revenue (€30k+ MRR or €500k+ ARR) who have outgrown founder-led growth but cannot yet afford an in-house VP Growth (€120–160k fully-loaded). They need a growth function, not a growth hire. Why: this segment has the problem acutely (they're actively losing to more-resourced competitors), can afford the €3.5–8k/month spend, and is small enough to be reachable through founder-community channels. Avoid: pre-revenue founders (wrong time), 50+ person companies (they'll hire in-house), and sector-specialist verticals (wrong depth).
R5 · Go-to-market wedge
Lead with the 43% GTM failure stat; lead with the Playbook as the signature artefact.
The 43% figure (CB Insights 2024, n=431 VC-backed shutdowns — startup failures that trace to poor PMF / GTM execution) is precise, sourced, and defensible. It sets up the problem without overclaiming market size. The Monthly Playbook is the signature artefact — the thing partners can hold up and say "this is what we produce." Everything else (dashboard, router, ALC menu) is load-bearing but invisible; the Playbook is the public face. Why: campaigns need a single emblem; founders trust artefacts more than marketing pages.

4. What this does NOT claim

We are not claiming PMF.
The recommendations above are pre-PMF positioning choices — how we enter the market. PMF is confirmed (or not) by the first three signed clients running full monthly cycles at full price. If fewer than 2 of the first 3 cohort-one clients would score "very disappointed" after 90 days, the positioning is wrong and we rebuild it.
We are not claiming the category is empty.
"Growth OS" is an uncrowded phrase but the work underneath it is adjacent to growth agencies, fractional VP-growth services, HubSpot partner agencies, and RevOps consultancies. The differentiator is the product surface (the Monthly Playbook + five tools), not the service shape.

6. Action items for go-live

  • Before 22 April soft launch: align all four partners on R1–R3 (category / tagline system / two-stream revenue). R4–R5 can be tested against cohort-one without prior alignment.
  • Month 1 of cohort-one: instrument the Sean Ellis survey inside the Growth Dashboard. Fire at day 45, 90, 120.
  • Month 3 of cohort-one: first positioning retrospective. Keep / kill / iterate each of R1–R5 based on signal.
  • Month 6: if any of the three failure tests fires, convene a positioning working session with partners. Do not wait for month 12.
