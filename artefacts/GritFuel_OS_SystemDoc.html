Context

The Problem — and the Answer

The Problem

Most growth agencies operate as one-off project shops. Each month starts from scratch: no memory, no compounding effect, no system.

Clients move between agencies chasing tactics. Budgets are allocated by gut feel. Briefs land on the wrong desks. Performance data sits in dashboards nobody reads.

The result: growth that is random, expensive, and impossible to attribute.

Most agencies reset every month.
GritFuel compounds every month.
The Opportunity

Brands that compound — building on last month's data to inform this month's strategy — grow faster, waste less budget, and retain agency relationships longer.

The GritFuel OS is our proprietary system for making this happen at scale, across every client, every month.

A retainer model built on credits.

A system, not a service.
The GritFuel OS links onboarding intelligence to dashboard tracking to compounding recommendations to credit allocation — every month, automatically.

Tool 03

Compounding Engine

Last month's performance becomes next month's playbook — automatically

The Compounding Engine is the intelligence layer of the OS. It reads this month's channel performance, compares it to last month, checks milestone progress, and scores four signals to produce a Compound Score and a ranked list of recommended initiatives for the following month.

Channel Momentum
30%
Measures month-over-month growth per active channel. Fast-growing channels receive more weight in the score. Stagnant channels are flagged for review or reallocation.
Goal Proximity
25%
Distance from the client's primary metric target. The closer to goal, the higher the urgency to sustain. Far from target triggers rebalancing recommendations.
Credit Efficiency
25%
Output growth generated per credit spent, per channel. Identifies best-performing and worst-performing credit deployments across the portfolio.
Dependency Sequencing
20%
Tracks which of 12 milestones across 3 tiers have been completed. Completed foundations automatically unlock higher-tier initiatives in the recommendation set.
Compound Score → Strategy
75 – 100
ACCELERATE
Double down on what's working. Push credits into top-performing channels.
55 – 74
MAINTAIN
Hold course. Optimise efficiency within current channel mix.
35 – 54
REBALANCE
Shift credits away from underperformers. Test adjacent channels.
0 – 34
PIVOT
Fundamental rethink required. Escalate to senior strategy.
How it connects
The Compound Score feeds directly into the Escalation Router (PIVOT scores trigger escalation) and the Credit Allocation tool (ACCELERATE scores concentrate credits; REBALANCE scores redistribute them).

Tool 04

Escalation & Handoff Router

Every brief routed to the right partner — and the right brief written for each audience

When a brief arrives, the Router decides who handles it and at what escalation level — based on four live inputs. It then generates two versions of the brief: an internal handoff note (naming partners and scores) and a sanitised client-facing brief (using role descriptions only).

The 4 Trigger Types
Service Category
Baseline
19 service tags pre-weighted to a default partner (content/SEO/PR → GritFuel; brand/creative → brand specialists; paid/data/tech → performance team; events → activations team).
Complexity Flags
+Score
8 checkboxes — multi-market, regulatory, tech build, etc. Each adds directly to the relevant specialist's score.
Client Signals
+Urgency
7 behavioural cues: churn risk, growth moment, exec escalation, and others. High-signal briefs are flagged for senior review.
Compound Trigger
+Direction
Current Compound Score quartile feeds directly into routing. PIVOT scores escalate automatically; ACCELERATE briefs may stay with GritFuel.
Escalation Levels
None
GritFuel handles the full brief. No specialist involvement required.
Soft handoff
A specialist partner is briefed and contributes. GritFuel remains primary contact.
Full escalation
Specialist partner leads delivery. GritFuel coordinates and client-faces.
Multi-partner
Two or more specialist partners involved. A joint brief and credit split is produced.
Client language rule — always enforced
Partner names are never shared with clients. The router automatically substitutes: specialist partners become "our senior brand specialists", "our performance marketing team", "our events & activations team".
How it connects
Router output informs the Credit Allocation tool — escalated briefs carry higher credit requirements and partner cost splits.

Tool 05

Dynamic Credit Allocation

Live credit splits across partners and initiatives — with mid-month reallocation built in

The Credit Allocation tool is where the month is planned and replanned. Strategists set the monthly credit budget, add initiatives from the compounding engine's recommendations, allocate credits per initiative, and lock confirmed items. When a client pauses or changes direction, credits are reallocated in seconds.

Credit spinners

Lock & protect

Auto-balance

Status cycling

Mid-month mode

Commercial Model

How GritFuel Makes Money

A subscription model built for predictability, margin, and compounding client value
Retainer Credits
  • Clients subscribe to a monthly credit package.
  • Each credit = €25 of delivered output value.
  • Credits roll up to the GritFuel OS dashboard.
  • Minimum engagement: 10 credits / month (€250).
  • Typical client: 20–40 credits / month.
Partner Margin
  • GritFuel orchestrates specialist partners.
  • Partner cost sits below the credit rate.
  • GritFuel retains the margin between the two.
  • Partners are invisible to clients — GritFuel brand only.
  • Target gross margin: 40–55% on partner-routed credits.
Paid Media Pass-through
  • Paid media is billed at cost — no markup.
  • This is a trust signal, not a revenue driver.
  • Keeps the model clean and client-aligned.
  • Media tracked in the dashboard; not in credit rate.
  • Positioned as a client benefit, not a concession.
