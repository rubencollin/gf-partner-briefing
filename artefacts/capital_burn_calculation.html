Capital Burn Calculation

How the "€15–25B wasted annually in W. Europe + SSA" figure reconstructs — and where it breaks.

The figure appears in two places (Context Doc §11, Investor Deck v1.1 market-gap slide) but without a derivation. This memo rebuilds it from publicly-cited anchors so we can decide whether to: (a) keep it as-is, (b) narrow it to a defensible sub-figure, or (c) drop it in favour of cleaner anchors.

1. What the figure is trying to say

"Every year, ~€15–25B of capital deployed across W. Europe and Sub-Saharan Africa ends up in ventures that never achieve traction. A large share of that failure traces to GTM / execution gaps — the specific problem GritFuel's OS addresses."

B. W. Europe — non-VC capital burn

Founder equity, angel, bootstrap, corporate-venture, and accelerator-backed ventures. Much harder to measure — no single registry. Typical proxy: non-VC early-stage capital is roughly 0.5–1.0x VC flow across mature ecosystems (OECD / Invest Europe methodology notes).

The honest range is €9–18B / yr — meaningfully lower than the €15–25B in the Context Doc. The original upper bound likely came from (a) a higher EU VC base assumption, (b) more generous non-VC capital assumptions, or (c) including 10–15yr cohort tails. None of those are unreasonable, but none are clean enough to defend under pressure.

4. The sharper, narrower claim

Not all failure burn is addressable by GritFuel. CB Insights 2024 (431 VC-backed shutdowns analysed) finds 43% of startup failures trace to poor PMF / GTM execution, followed by bad timing (29%) and unsustainable unit economics (19%). Applied to our range:

GritFuel-addressable wedge
43% × €9–18B = €3.9–7.7B / yr of failure burn in W. Europe + SSA is attributable to the specific gap GritFuel's OS closes — GTM execution infrastructure that founders never built.

5. Where this calculation breaks — trap list

Trap 1 — VC power-law is not "waste"
VCs expect 50–70% of portfolio to fail. That is the business model, not the market inefficiency. Citing it as waste to a VC audience will read as naïve.
Trap 2 — Correlation ≠ causation
43% of failures "trace to" GTM is not the same as "43% would have been saved by better GTM." Some would have failed anyway — wrong market, wrong moment, wrong founder. The truly addressable share is smaller.
Trap 3 — Double-counting risk
EU VC + non-VC + founder opportunity cost may overlap. A failed founder's €200k savings burn is already partly captured in the salary line.
Trap 4 — Audience mismatch
For founders, the figure reads as scary validation. For VCs, it reads as either a truism (they know) or an indictment (their portfolios fail). The framing has to flip by audience.

6. Recommendation

Don't anchor on "€15–25B wasted"
The number is directionally defensible but the upper bound is shaky and the framing invites pushback — especially from the VC audience we actively want in the room.
Do anchor on the narrower wedge
Founder-facing: "Thousands of bold ideas die every year across Europe and Africa — not from lack of product, but from lack of execution infrastructure. 43% of startup failures trace to GTM, not the idea." (Qualitative. Sources: CB Insights 2024.)

VC/accelerator-facing: "Portfolio mortality doesn't have to trace to GTM gaps. GritFuel is the execution layer that removes the 43% of failure modes founders can't self-solve." (Reframes the VC as solution-buyer, not waste-owner.)
