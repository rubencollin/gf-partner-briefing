Design decisions & rationale
Resolved — Hybrid
Price increases: Growth +€250 → €5,250 · Performance +€500 → €8,000
Modest increases to bring fixed-item margin above 30% across all tiers. Core stays at €3,500. Increases are below 10% and justified by the model depth (CMO oversight, weekly cadence, specialist involvement).

Resulting margins: Core 53% · Growth 42% · Performance 30% — all above the 30% threshold.
Resolved — Hybrid
Reclassified items: deliverables moved from fixed to credit-funded
Growth: Structured growth experiments + Multi-channel orchestration moved to credit-funded. Rationale: these are deliverable outputs, not relationship overhead — clients should direct credit spend here via the monthly playbook, not receive them as undifferentiated fixed inclusions.

Performance: Advanced testing architecture + Forecasting & growth modelling moved to credit-funded. This makes Performance's credit pool feel more powerful — clients get 180 credits to direct at high-value analytical work, not just production volume.
Rationale
Growth "Everything in Core, plus": Fractional CMO oversight only
With structured growth experiments and multi-channel orchestration moved to credit-funded, Fractional CMO oversight is the sole additional fixed inclusion in Growth. This is intentional: the CMO function is pure relationship and strategic oversight — it has no deliverable output by itself, which makes it genuinely "fixed." It's also the clearest signal of the tier upgrade: a senior lens applied to everything the client does.
Rationale
Performance "Everything in Growth, plus": Priority account access
After reclassification, Performance's structural differentiators are the cadence (4× weekly reviews, 6 hrs on-demand), the larger credit pool (180 credits), and the additional specialist (events & activation). "Priority account access & senior escalation" captures the one meaningful fixed addition: Performance clients get top-of-queue access to senior leadership when issues escalate. This is a commitment, not a deliverable — correctly classified as fixed.
Rationale
Session naming: strategy → alignment → growth review
Monthly strategy session (Core) — setting direction once a month. Bi-weekly alignment (Growth) — checking course mid-month, adjusting priorities. "Alignment" is more accurate than "strategy" at this cadence. Weekly growth review (Performance) — operational pulse checks at weekly frequency. "Review" is honest; "strategy" at 4× a month would set the wrong expectation.
Rationale
"Your credits fund" — illustrative examples per tier, 153-item menu linked
Each tier's three bullet examples are anchored to that tier's typical credit use. They are not an exhaustive list — the 153-item menu callout makes this explicit. Examples intentionally escalate across tiers: Core (audits, brand basics) → Growth (experiments, multi-channel) → Performance (testing architecture, forecasting) to reinforce tier positioning without restricting what clients can actually buy.
