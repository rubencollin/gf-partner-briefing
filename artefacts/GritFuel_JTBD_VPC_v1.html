Stress Test — Where This Is Soft
The point of this exercise is to break the canvas before prospects do. Here's where I'd push.
Principle

A value proposition is only strong if it survives hostile questioning. The six assumptions below are the ones I'd interrogate first with real prospects — if any of them break, the canvas doesn't just need a tweak, it needs a rewrite.

Weakest assumptions

The six things most likely to break
1 Founders will pay for a system they can't see.
The OS is 5 tools — but tools aren't emotional; growth is. Why sign €3.5–8k/mo for a system vs. 2 fractional people they can Zoom with? The Compound Score and Dashboard must be the emotional hook, not the back-office. Right now they're described as outputs, not as the product.
2 Partners stay invisible and cooperative at 75% cost share.
As GritFuel scales, .frank / Bluegyfu / BFL notice their labor is the backbone. This is a pyramid held together by relationships. What happens when Bluegyfu goes direct to a client they delivered for? The competitive moat is behind someone else's door — the single biggest structural risk in the model.
3 SMEs with €50–500k budgets want a system, not a relationship.
EU mid-market buying behavior is deeply relationship-driven. The OS is the rational argument but the human/trust layer closes the deal. Do target founders actually want a "system"? Or do they want Ruben and the founders, with the system as proof they'll deliver?
4 The credit model reduces friction.
It may add cognitive load. "What is a credit? How much is a blog post? Did I use my 70 this month?" Every budget-minded client will see credits as abstraction disguised as transparency. Test with actual prospects — do they find it clarifying or confusing?
5 EU and SSA share a JTBD.
Cultural and structural buying norms differ. The same canvas won't work for both — which is partly why this one is EU-only. But when GTM activates in SSA, plan for a parallel canvas, not a translation.
6 No agency can copy this.
S4 Capital, WPP's SME arms, M&C Saatchi, Dept — any of them could copy the OS model in 12–18 months once it's validated. The data-accumulation moat is real but slow (6–12 months per client). Head start > inherent defensibility.

Missing pains

What the canvas doesn't yet capture
+ Procurement & legal friction
SMEs above 50 FTE have procurement processes. One monthly subscription invoice is a selling feature — but only if the MSA, DPA, and SLA are frictionless. None of this shows up in current GTM narrative. Add it.
+ Switching-away-from-incumbent pain
Many target SMEs are 12–24 months into a 36-month agency contract. The pain of leaving is as real as the pain of staying. Need an explicit "migration playbook" offer.
+ Board reporting pain
Founders don't just want growth — they want narratable growth for board decks. The Dashboard needs a one-click board-ready export. Not implied in current product description.
+ Internal politics with HoG
When the founder signs without looping in the HoG, the HoG blocks delivery from day one. GritFuel needs an explicit co-pilot narrative for the HoG — not just empty "we empower you."
+ Founder's own credibility pain
Many founders hire agencies partly so they can blame them when growth is slow. GritFuel's "one throat to choke" model concentrates accountability — which is a feature for rational CEOs, a risk for anxious ones. Know which you're selling to.

Five questions for the first ten prospect conversations

These will validate or break the canvas faster than any desk research
  1. When was the last time you changed marketing vendor — and what, specifically, triggered the switch?
  2. If we took GritFuel away from you tomorrow, what would you fall back to? Walk me through day one.
  3. What would you miss first: the strategy, the execution, or the data?
  4. Which month were you closest to cancelling? What saved it?
  5. Who have you told about us — and what exactly did you say?
