Risk Register · What we're honest about not knowing

The things that could break this.

Seven named risks ranked by exposure. Each has a likelihood, an impact, a short mitigation, and an owner. The top risk — product-engineering capability — is the one that converts the OS story into software over time, and it's a strategic conversation we owe each other rather than a problem to outrun by shipping harder.

Risk #1 · High · Product-engineering capability gap

The OS is positioned as product, but nobody owns the product-engineering build yet.

The story says "Growth Operating System." That's a software claim — a client-facing Dashboard running live, an Escalation Router automating handoffs, a Compounding Engine picking next plays, an ALC Menu where credits resolve into delivered work. Each is an engineering surface. Right now the OS exists as a well-specified set of HTML prototypes and spec docs — not code a client logs into. The gap is small at five beta clients and material at fifty.

Client-facing product engineering is a new capability the OS positioning asks of us — outside the original partnership's service shape. Existing shareholdings stay where they are; this is a new role to discuss, and the question is what shape the hire takes and how the cost is funded. Full framing — three lanes, shareholding impact, hire options — on the capability map.

Build order once the role is in seat: (1) harden the OS as described — Dashboard, Intake, Compounding Engine, Escalation Router, Credit Allocation — to a production standard. (2) Automate the ALC Menu — moves Stream 2 margin from "75% cost-share to partners" to actual productised margin. Step 1 sits in the product-engineering lane and needs a dedicated owner; step 2 sits naturally in the automation lane.

Likelihood: High if unresolved by Q3 Impact: Shapes product scalability past 25–50 clients Owner: Ruben + All partners Mitigation: Agree split; scope hire path by 20 April decision sync
02

Delivery partner capacity under unexpected demand

Stream 2 runs on .frank / Bluegyfu / BFL capacity at 75% cost-share. If cohort one converts faster than modeled (5+ clients in the first month), we hit a ceiling on specialist hours before we hit a ceiling on revenue. The Capacity Plan assumes capacity scales; it doesn't commit any of the three partners to a specific bench size.

Likelihood
Medium-high
Impact
High — service quality drop risks beta reputation
Mitigation
Formal capacity commitment from each partner before 18 May. Bench size + SLA per tier.
Owner
Ruben + all partners
03

Credit-rollover cashflow under churn

3-month credit rollover sounds customer-friendly, but under churn it stacks liability: a client who pays for 90 credits over 3 months and cancels can still demand delivery on 90 credits after cancellation. The Fixed-Cost Model assumes steady-state — it doesn't model the liability build under a 20% monthly churn scenario.

Likelihood
Medium
Impact
High — cash crunch in month 4–6 if churn spikes
Mitigation
Contract clause: unused credits expire on cancellation OR are convertible to a final deliverable. Choose one.
Owner
Ruben · needs legal review by 26 April
04

SA market entry sequencing

Running NL + SA in parallel for cohort one doubles the operating surface (timezones, currencies, delivery partner coordination, contract localisation) while the team is at its smallest. The sizing work supports SA as a market; it doesn't mandate SA in cohort one. Decision 05 resolves this, but the risk is that "parallel" becomes the default by inertia without being chosen deliberately.

Likelihood
High (of inertia, not of the strategy itself)
Impact
Medium — slows NL execution without materially accelerating SA
Mitigation
Force the call in the Mon 20 April sync. Default to NL-only unless deliberately chosen otherwise.
Owner
Tlali + all partners · Decision 05
05

Capital-burn anchor fragility

The "€15–25B wasted annually" figure in the Context Doc and Investor Deck v1.1 has no defensible derivation. The reconstructed honest range is €9–18B. The gap invites pushback from a VC audience who knows the underlying data — precisely the audience we want in the room. Not blocking, but worth fixing before the next investor conversation.

Likelihood
Medium (high if a VC does the math live)
Impact
Medium — credibility hit in one deck, recoverable
Mitigation
Per Capital Burn memo: swap aggregate for the 43% PMF/GTM stat. Keep the €45B deployment figure as context only.
Owner
Ruben · fix in Investor Deck v1.2
06

Pricing transparency trap

Decision 04 (surface credits publicly?) has a second-order risk regardless of which way it goes. Surface them: anchors sales calls lower and attracts price shoppers. Don't surface them: lengthens the sales cycle because prospects can't self-qualify. The split is real; either choice creates a workstream downstream.

Likelihood
Medium
Impact
Medium — affects cohort-one conversion rate
Mitigation
A/B test in month two — run both versions to subsets of outbound list, measure call-book rate.
Owner
All · Decision 04
07

Category-claim contest

"Growth Operating System" is a fresh category but not an uncontested one. Adjacent players (Mutiny, Common Room, Default, North Star metric tooling, the RevOps category generally) can claim operating-system language without much friction. If a better-funded entrant plants a flag in the same phrase while we're still in beta, the category advantage compresses.

Likelihood
Medium (higher if we stay quiet past Q3)
Impact
Medium — positioning erosion, not product erosion
Mitigation
Public "what a Growth OS actually is" content track from May. Own the definition before competitors attempt to.
Owner
Kim + Ruben

How this register works

Reviewed monthly. Risks move down the list as they resolve; new ones get added when they surface. The top risk (the product-engineering scope gap) stays at #1 until the capability split is agreed, a hire path is chosen, and a first engineer is ramping. It sits higher than the others because most of the others are fixable with a decision; this one is fixable with a decision plus a capability build.
