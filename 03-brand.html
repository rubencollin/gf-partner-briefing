Section 03 · Brand

Same soul. Sharper system.

Brand Guide v2 doesn't throw out the 2025 guide — it builds on it. The voice is the same. The colours are the same. What's new is a tighter application system, a productised tone, and a couple of open questions on type that genuinely need the three of you.

The shape

Two layers of the same guide.

The brand operates on two layers. An identity system — logo, palette, voice, positioning verbs — earned and stable. An application system that specifies how the identity behaves across a product surface, a dashboard, an intake form, a playbook PDF, a sales deck, a LinkedIn post. Both sit inside one guide, and both are needed now that the product surface has expanded.

IDENTITY LAYER Identity system

Logo, charcoal + lime + orange palette, display/body type pairing, voice principles, photographic style. Umbrella brand line: HUMAN THINKING. RELENTLESS EXECUTION.

Identity-led · Load-bearing · Stays put

APPLICATION LAYER Application system

Voice matrix by surface (founder-facing vs operator-facing vs public), layout grids for the product UI, rules for the Monthly Playbook, data-viz palette, illustration guidelines, and a motion primer.

Application-led · Productises the identity

What's locked

Logo. Core palette. Voice principles. Positioning verbs. "Human thinking. Relentless execution." stays as the umbrella brand line — earned, on the site, still working. The application layer extends it rather than replacing it.

The one real tension

Serif display on the site. Sans display in v2.

The live site (gritfuel.works) uses a high-contrast serif for display type — editorial, confident, a little old-money. Brand Guide v2 is all Montserrat — cleaner, more systematic, better for product surfaces. Both are defensible. They're just not the same answer.

This is the single biggest brand call in front of us. It shows up everywhere: the site hero, the Monthly Playbook cover, the dashboard chrome, the pitch deck, the LinkedIn templates. Picking one and committing it is worth more than hedging.

Key decisions

What's locked, what's open.

Core palette — locked

Charcoal, lime, orange, paper, gray. No changes. These carry from the 2025 guide and through the site, product, playbook, and dashboard.

Locked

Voice principles — locked

Signal over noise. Structure over chaos. Compounding over sprints. Still the three pillars the voice rests on. v2 adds how those show up by surface.

Locked

Display type — serif (site today) or Montserrat (v2)?

Editorial-serif feels premium and distinctive but is harder to apply in product UI. Montserrat is more functional across surfaces but risks feeling SaaS-generic. This is the biggest call.

Open

Tagline — a three-layer system (locked)

Umbrella: "Human thinking. Relentless execution." — the brand line. Stays.
Category descriptor: "The growth operating system." — pairs with the name on first contact to defuse the petrol-brand confusion (a lot of people hear "GritFuel" and assume fuel/energy/performance brand). The descriptor does the disambiguation work the name can't.
Product companion: "Growth that compounds. Visibly." — the GritFuel OS companion line. Replaces the older four-word rhythm ("Build. Test. Optimize. Win.") wherever it still appears in downstream artefacts.

Rule of thumb: first time someone meets the brand, lead with name + category descriptor. Once they know what we are, the umbrella and companion carry the rest.

Locked

Photography — parallelogram team treatment, keep or retire?

The slanted parallelogram mask on team photography is distinctive but it's fiddly to apply at scale. v2 quietly retires it in favour of a soft-edge portrait style. This is a strategic decision, not just a stylistic one. Portraits are how a prospect meets their Strategist before onboarding — on the website, in the pitch deck, in the first email. The named-Strategist model only works if the Strategist is visible. Whichever treatment we land on has to carry that weight consistently across surfaces. Preference?
