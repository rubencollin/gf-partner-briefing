Pipeline Plan · Cohort One

Honest page. Zero conversations, zero warm intros, zero LOIs, nothing in a CRM. Any "numbers" would be invented. What follows isn't a funnel — it's the short list of things that have to resolve between now and 18 May for a cohort-one pipeline to form at all. Prerequisites · in order

What has to exist before a pipeline can.

Six gates, five of which are open decisions the group hasn't made. Until they resolve, any outbound is speculative — the positioning, the geo, the owner, and the tracking don't exist yet.

Gate 01

Decide geography: NL-only, or NL + SA in parallel?

Shapes the outbound list. You cannot write a target list without knowing whether it's a list of one country or two. NL €500k–€5m and SA R5M–R100M is the current working hypothesis but has not been committed by the group.

Owner: Tlali lead · all in
→ Decision 05
Gate 02

Decide who does the outbound.

Partner rotation, junior BDR hire, or results-only outbound partner. Each commits different capacity and cost. No outbound happens until this is called — and that directly determines whether cohort one signs in May or July.

Owner: all partners
→ Decision 06
Gate 03

Decide the beachhead shape — narrow or horizontal?

Consumer DTC and vertical SaaS are the two working candidates. Narrow cohort-one means cleaner positioning and faster pattern-matching; horizontal means broader signal and slower learning. Partners have strong priors here and they diverge — worth surfacing now, not at month three.

Owner: all partners · post-launch review
→ Decision 07
Gate 04

Stand up CRM tracking before any first call.

HubSpot Starter or Attio — the call doesn't matter, the existence does. Tracking outbound in a spreadsheet past three conversations is how intelligence disappears. Stage, owner, next action, decline reason — all of that has to be captured from call one.

Owner: Ruben
By: 27 April 2026
Gate 05

Activate partner-referral channels from day one.

The 60/30/10 split allocates ~30% of cohort one to partner-led referrals. Tlali and Kim's networks are the first expression of that. "Activation" means a named list each of five people you would actually introduce us to, held in the CRM, worked through in May. Not a vibe — a list.

Owner: Tlali + Kim
By: 18 May 2026
Gate 06

Agree what "cohort one" is supposed to prove.

Before we talk conversion, we need to agree what conversion would even mean. Cohort one is a learning vehicle, not a revenue vehicle — it has to produce data across the tier mix, and at least one client who uses the credit pool the way the model assumes. That's the success definition; the target size follows.

Owner: all partners · before 18 May
Review mid-May.
Cohort-one target shape

What we're aiming for — and what it would have to look like.

These are working assumptions. None of them are proven yet; they shape the outbound once the gates above resolve.

Cohort-one size
3–5 signed by end of May; 6–8 as stretch. Smaller than instinct says — cohort one is a learning run, not a revenue run. Each client generates more signal than any tier-mix model predicts.
Tier mix
Aim for at least one in each tier — Core, Growth, Performance. Without that, we don't get signal on whether the Hybrid cost model holds across tiers. See Fixed-Cost Model.
Geo split
Resolves with Decision 05. If NL + SA: 3:2 working ratio. If NL-only: all five NL, SA phases in at cohort two.
Source mix
60% founder-direct · 30% partner-referral · 10% inbound. The partner-referral share only materialises if Gate 05 lands. Otherwise the model quietly defaults to 100% founder-direct — which it shouldn't.
Conversion assumption
Unknown. We have no cohort-derived conversion rate — anything we quote would be a guess. First real data lands after the first five intake calls.
First measurable moment
End of May. Not "signed revenue" — that's the wrong bar. The real bar is: did anyone engage an intake call, did any partner refer anyone, and did the CRM catch it?

The honest read.

Zero is an honest number. A pipeline doesn't exist yet because the prerequisites don't exist yet — and the prerequisites are the six decisions this briefing is built around. If the partner group resolves Gates 01–03 by soft launch (22 April) and commits to Gates 04–06 during the inner-circle window before 18 May, cohort one is reachable in June. If those gates slip, cohort one slips with them — and every week of slippage compresses the measurement window before the first iteration of the product has to land.
