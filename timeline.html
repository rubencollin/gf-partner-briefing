Timeline · Reverse-engineered from 18 May

29 days. Four weeks with one job each.

We don't have to call before 22 April. This page reverse-engineers what needs to happen — week by week — to put us in a position to personally approach named prospects on 18 May. Each week does one thing. Decisions lock early so the build phase can move fast and the warm-up phase can be boring.

The shape of 29 days

Phase 1 is what we've built to date. The four weeks that follow are the recalibration. Launch day is one day.

Between now and 22 April, no new material. Critical fixes only, so the inner circle meets a site that's sharp but honest about where we are. The 22 April release is the conversation-starter, not the finished product.

Phase 2 — Four weeks, one job each

Sequenced so that decisions happen before building, and building finishes before warming. If we slip a week, we don't slip the launch — we slip what we ship.

Week 1
22 – 28 Apr
Listen.
Each partner holds 3–5 inner-circle conversations. We shut up and take notes. No public signal yet — if we broadcast this week we lose the data we came for.
  • Daily feedback capture into a shared doc — one row per conversation, themes tagged
  • No public posts from partners or company page
  • End-of-week synthesis (Friday 24 Apr): what shifted, what held, what breaks
Owners · All four partners · Ruben synthesises Friday
Week 2
29 Apr – 5 May
Decide.
Six calls lock by EOW 5 May. These gate Week 3 — if any slip, Week 3 starts empty-handed. Partner review of the locked set on Fri 1 May, sign-off on Mon 5 May.
  • Pricing approach — public, gated, or "from €X"
  • Lead-capture offer — discovery sprint / waitlist / newsletter
  • Outbound mix — LinkedIn DMs + partner emails to ICPs; tooling, cadence, voice
  • ICP shortlist — 30–50 named accounts with owners assigned
  • Partner email approach — who emails whom, signature, one-pager attached?
  • Strategist booking path — what a prospect actually books
  • Positioning shifts surfaced by inner circle — fold in or hold
Owners · Partner group decision · Ruben drives the doc
Week 3
6 – 12 May
Build.
Decisions are locked; scope is finite. Monday 6 May starts with a 60-min web-team brief. Friday 12 May everything is technically done — the weekend is for sleep, not shipping.
  • Mon 6 May — brief web team on v2: site updates, pricing page (if public), lead-capture, booking
  • GritFuel LinkedIn company page live — banner, About, pinned post, first 3 posts queued
  • Partner announcement posts drafted, peer-reviewed, scheduled
  • Partner email templates — one per sender style; one-pager PDF locked
  • Email deliverability check — SPF / DMARC on partner domains; test-send to a colleague
  • CRM hygiene — ICP list imported, tagged, owners assigned
  • Booking link tested end-to-end from a cold browser
Owners · Ruben (web, company page) · Each partner (own posts, own emails)
Week 4
13 – 17 May
Warm.
Partners start showing up on LinkedIn — industry takes, early opinions, not yet the announcement. Final deploys happen mid-week so we have 48h of air cover before launch day.
  • Wed 13 May — partners begin personal LinkedIn warming posts (no announcement yet)
  • Thu 14 May — final web build deployed to production
  • Fri 15 May — internal dry run: walk a fake prospect through the funnel end-to-end
  • Sat 16 May — final QA: every link, template, tracking pixel, booking slot
  • Sun 17 May — everything locked; nothing ships
Owners · Each partner on posts · Ruben on build + QA

Phase 3 — Launch day

One day. One sequence. If Weeks 1–4 did their job, 18 May is calm.

18
May
Official launch · T0
First day of targeted outbound
09:00 CET — partners publish announcement posts simultaneously. GritFuel company page publishes its launch post. 10:00–12:00 — first batch of 10–15 named-prospect DMs and personal partner emails go out. Afternoon — monitor replies, queue follow-ups for Day 2. Booking system live. Not a mass blast; a warm, coordinated opening move.

Two forks drive everything else

The rest of Week 3 depends on how these land. If they slip past Week 2, Week 3 starts with guesses instead of specs.

Fork 1 · Outbound channel mix
LinkedIn DMs and partner emails
Confirmed. Determines Week 3 build: one email template per partner voice, a locked one-pager PDF, deliverability sanity checks on partner domains, and LinkedIn post schedules per partner. No automation, no sequences — personal outreach only on Day 1.
Fork 2 · Pricing visibility
Public page, gated, or "from €X"?
Open. This one changes the Week 3 web brief scope more than any other decision — a public pricing page is 2–3 days of design and copy; a gated one is half a day. Decide early in Week 2 so the web team isn't briefed with a shrug.

Assumptions — flag if any shift

These are the four premises the timeline is built on. If one moves, the plan moves with it — better to flag in Week 1 than discover in Week 4.

Confirmed with partner group

  • 18 May is first-day outbound, not a mass blast. Day 1 volume ≈ 10–15 warmed accounts.
  • LinkedIn DMs + partner emails to named ICPs is the channel mix. No cold email lists, no paid ads.
  • Website is public-facing on 18 May — not a gated microsite.
  • Inner-circle feedback shapes the refinement, but does not move the 18 May date unless something is structurally broken.
