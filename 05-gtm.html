Section 05 · GTM

How we find cohort one.

The strategy, the product, the brand, and the money all point inward — at what we're building. GTM points outward: at the first ten founders who let us run the OS against their growth reality. This section is about how we get there by 18 May, and what we learn from it.

The shape

GTM as its own discipline.

Client acquisition is treated as a discipline, not a side effect of brand equity. Two axes: explicit assumptions mapped to testable tactics, and a first cohort recruited intentionally to pressure-test each assumption. Inbound stays — it's how relationships convert — but it's no longer the whole plan.

THE METHOD Assumption-led outbound + inbound

Explicit assumptions about who buys, why, when, and through which channel — each mapped to a testable tactic and a falsifier. Inbound continues via partner networks and referrals; outbound adds a deliberate, cohort-focused motion on top.

Intentional · Testable · Learning-compounding

THE COHORT First ten, recruited intentionally

~10 founders across NL + SA, recruited against the ICP bands, with a working onboarding flow ready to hit at the 18 May official launch. Each signing tests one or more assumptions in the map below.

Cohort-anchored · Cross-market · Launch-ready

Cohort one is a learning asset, not a revenue asset

The first ten aren't priced to maximise revenue — they're priced to maximise what we learn about delivery, tooling, escalation, and positioning. Every assumption in the map gets tested through them. That framing matters for how we recruit and what we promise.

What a cohort-one founder actually signs for

The product a cohort-one founder signs for is a Strategist with a name on it — introduced at onboarding, present each month, accountable to the outcome. Everything else — the five tools, the Playbook ritual, the credit mechanic — is how that Strategist keeps the promise at cadence. The pitch sells the system; the engagement is held by a person.

From assumption to tactic

Ten assumptions, ten tactics, one cohort.

The Assumption-to-Tactic map is the spine. Each assumption (e.g. "post-seed founders will pay monthly for a system, not a service") has a tactic ("targeted outbound to founders of €1m – €5m revenue consumer DTCs; pitch the Playbook not the team") and a falsifier ("if conversion to discovery call is under 8% across 100 attempts, the assumption is wrong").

Cohort one runs across the first 90 days post official launch. By August we'll know which assumptions held, which broke, and which we mis-specified.

Key decisions

What's locked, what's open.

Cohort-one framing — learning cohort, not revenue cohort

First ten clients are priced, scoped, and recruited to maximise signal. We say that to them openly. It makes partner conversations easier.

Locked

Assumption-to-tactic map as the GTM operating doc

Every tactic has an assumption behind it and a falsifier. No un-attributed activity. Updated monthly based on cohort-one signal.

Locked

Channel weighting — outbound, inbound, or partner-led for cohort one?

All three work. The question is where we spend scarce founder-time. My read: 60% outbound, 30% partner-led, 10% content inbound. Push back.

Open

Who runs outbound — us, or an external partner?

Cohort-one outbound will eat hours. Options: a partner rotates in; we hire a junior BDR on day one; we use an outbound partner on a results-only arrangement. All three have costs.

Open

Onboarding flow — founder-only or founder + team?

The Onboarding Intake is written for the founder. Most of our target companies have a head of growth or head of marketing too. Do we insist on both being in the intake, or keep it founder-only and invite the team in post-signature?

What I need from you

Three specific questions on GTM.

  1. Channel weighting: is my 60/30/10 split (outbound / partner-led / content inbound) for cohort one the right shape? If not — what moves?
  2. Outbound ownership: a partner rotates in, we hire a junior BDR, or we use an outbound partner on results-only? Each has a cost the group has to accept.
  3. Intake participants: founder-only (simpler, faster) or founder + growth lead (more work to schedule, better brief)?
